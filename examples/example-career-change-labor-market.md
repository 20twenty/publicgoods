# Labor Market Snapshot for Career Changers

## Query

> "I am considering a career change — use FRED data to help me get perspective as I consider my options."

## FRED Data Series Used

*Based on the query, Claude selected the following FRED data series to pull and analyze:*

| Series | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| `UNRATE` | Unemployment rate (seasonally adjusted) — overall job market health |
| `JTSQUR` | Quits rate — share of workers voluntarily leaving jobs; the best proxy for worker confidence |
| `AHETPI` (pc1) | Average hourly earnings, year-over-year % change — wage growth trend |
| `JTSHIR` | Hire rate — how aggressively companies are bringing on new workers |
| `JTSJOL` | Job openings level — total unfilled positions in the economy |
| `RECPROUSM156N` | Recession probability — statistical likelihood of recession in the current month |

## Output

### Unemployment: 4.3% (stable)

The rate has been in the 4.1–4.5% band for the past year. Historically moderate — not a hot market, not a crisis. Unlikely to land in a hiring freeze, but also unlikely to have employers chasing you.

### The Quits Rate: 1.9% — the most telling number

The **quits rate** measures the share of workers voluntarily leaving their jobs. It's the best proxy for worker confidence in the job market.

| Period | Quits Rate |
|---|---|
| Pre-COVID avg (2016–2019) | 2.2% |
| Great Resignation peak (Nov 2021) | 3.0% |
| Today (Feb 2026) | 1.9% |

At 1.9%, workers are *less* confident switching jobs than they were pre-COVID. The window of unusual worker leverage is closed. Career changes still happen — they just take more preparation than they did in 2021–2022.

### Hiring Rate: 3.1% — slightly softer

Hiring slowed from ~3.4% through most of 2025 to 3.1% recently. Companies are still filling roles, but less aggressively.

### Job Openings: ~6.9M — back to normal

Down from a 12M peak in 2022, but roughly in line with pre-COVID levels (~7M). Openings exist; competition for them is higher.

### Wage Growth: ~3.4% YoY and slowing

Was running at 4.1% a year ago. Still positive in real terms (assuming ~2.5% inflation), but the leverage to negotiate large salary jumps during a switch has diminished.

### Recession Risk: Low (0.48% probability, Feb 2026)

There was a spike to 0.82% in October 2025 that has since faded. No recession signal right now.

---

### Takeaway

This is a **"prepare carefully, then move"** market — not a freeze, not a frenzy. The data doesn't say *don't do it*, it says the days of firing off 5 applications and getting 5 offers are over. A career change is most viable right now if you can:

1. **Target specifically** — openings exist but selectivity is up
2. **Bridge skills** — wage leverage comes from bringing something clear to a new role
3. **Time it well** — recession risk is low now, but if it rises, new hires are first cut
